🍝 Spaghetti Carbonara 🍝

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish originating from Rome. It's known for its creamy sauce made with eggs, cheese, and guanciale (cured pork), creating a rich and delicious meal that's surprisingly simple to prepare.

📋 Ingredients

  • 🍝400g spaghetti
  • 🍝200g guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 🍝4 large eggs
  • 🍝100g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 🍝100g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 🍝Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 🍝Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

👨‍🍳 Cooking Method

  1. Boil water: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente.
  2. Cook meat: While the pasta cooks, fry the diced guanciale in a large pan over medium heat until the fat is rendered and the meat is crispy (about 5 minutes).
  3. Prepare sauce: In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper until well combined.
  4. Reserve water: Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining the spaghetti.
  5. Combine: Add the hot drained pasta to the pan with the guanciale and remove from heat.
  6. Finish: Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, stirring constantly and adding pasta water a little at a time to create a creamy sauce.
  7. Serve: Serve immediately in warm bowls, garnished with fresh parsley and extra cheese if desired.

Buon Appetito! Enjoy your authentic Italian pasta! 🇮🇹
